Debriefing

Thank you very much for taking part in our study. In the following, you can read more about the purpose of this research as well as the specific hypotheses investigated.

The purpose of this research was to examine the relationship between bicultural individual's salience of death-related thoughts and their reaction to cultural stimuli. During the first survey, we measured the extent to which you identify as bicultural. We then induced thoughts of either death or dental pain to affect the degree to which thoughts about your identity were relevant to you and how much you would hold on to them. Then, you saw a series of images that were related to both of your cultural identities. By measuring the time, it took you to respond to the dots, we were able to measure the degree to which you paid attention to each identity.

We hypothesized that participants who were induced with death-related thoughts and were made more salient of one of their identities through language would pay more attention to one of their cultural identities. These findings will expand our understanding of how people form and hold on to cultural identities.

Thank you very much for your participation.

本研究是为了检验双重文化个体中死亡相关想法的凸显程度与这些个体对于文化刺激的反应之间的关系。第一份问卷考察您双重文化身份认同的程度。之后通过诱导出与死亡或者牙痛相关想法的方式，去影响您身份认同方面的想法与自我的关联程度，以及您保持这些想法的程度。接下来您看到一系列与您的两种文化身份均有关联的图片，通过测量您对圆点刺激的反应时，能够得知您对于每种文化身份的注意程度。

研究假设当被试被诱导出死亡相关的想法，并且通过语言体现出其中一种文化身份的凸显性时，将会把注意力更多的放在这一文化身份上面。这些结果将会拓展我们对于人们是如何形成并保持文化身份这一问题的理解。
